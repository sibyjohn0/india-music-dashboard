How to approach this list + budget guide ▼
Start here (free, highest ROI)

Email the 16 editorial blogs and 3 key podcasts (Maed in India, Wild City Podcast, The Music Run Down) first. These are Indian indie-specific, free to contact, and a placement here carries more weight than 50 playlist adds. Keep the pitch short: one paragraph on the track, one line on why this outlet specifically, a streaming link and a press photo. No attachments.

Expected conversion: 1 in 8 pitches gets a response. Budget: time only.

SubmitHub: south-asian curators first (17 curators, ~$25-40)

Filter to South Asian tagged above. These 17 curators have explicitly opted into this genre. Submit to all of them before going broader. Cost is 1-3 credits per submission (~$1-3 each). Enable the premium tier where available. Standard (free) submissions go to a lower-priority queue and often expire unheard.

Realistic budget for this tier: $25-40 total.

SubmitHub: genre-matched global curators (~$80-150 for a real push)

Filter by the genres closest to your sound (folk, indie-pop, singer-songwriter, lo-fi, world music). Prioritise curators with a high reach score and multiple Spotify playlists: they're more likely to place your track somewhere it gets streamed, not just saved. Skip curators at 4-5 credits unless their reach score is above 80: the ROI drops sharply at that price.

A focused campaign targeting 50-70 genre-matched curators will cost $80-150. That is a reasonable single-release budget for an emerging artist.

Do not blanket-submit to everything. SubmitHub tracks your acceptance rate: submitting to wrong-fit curators lowers your standing on the platform over time.

What to expect
  • SubmitHub approval rates across the platform average around 10-20%. A well-targeted campaign with good genre fit gets closer to 20-30%.
  • A Spotify playlist placement adds streams but rarely breaks an artist alone. The editorial coverage (Wild City, Indian Music Diaries) does more for long-term discovery.
  • YouTube reviewers are the highest single-video impact. One reaction video from a channel with 50K subscribers can outperform 30 playlist placements in terms of actual listener conversion. That tier is coming once the YouTube quota refreshes.
