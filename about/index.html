Why we built this
The system was never designed for most artists.
Siby John & Karishma Changroth

Two of us built this, Siby John and Karishma Changroth, from the two sides of the gap most independent artists fall into: the business they were never taught, and the identity they have not yet crystallised. The tools here are free and always will be.

Siby spent thirteen years inside the platforms that decide which music gets seen. Between 2022 and 2025, as Marketing Manager at YouTube India, he ran creator and music programmes with 400+ artists, labels, and production houses, including Anuv Jain, Jasleen Royal, and Jassie Gill. He is now Senior Creator Manager at LinkedIn. That work gave him a direct view of how the system actually surfaces music versus how artists assume it works. The gap was large. The artists genuinely building something had almost no reliable information about how the business functioned.

Karishma Changroth is Founding Member at Talented, Bangalore's independent creative agency. She brings brand identity, social strategy, and creative positioning: the half of the gap that is not about economics but about how an artist is perceived, packaged, and remembered. Together with Siby, the programme covers both sides of what blocks most independent artists: the business logic and the creative identity.

The economics are harder than most people say. A single finished song costs roughly ₹35,000 once you factor in studio time, mixing, mastering, and session musicians. A video adds ₹15,000 to ₹75,000. An artist releasing four songs a year is spending ₹2-3 lakh before they've made a rupee back. Streaming from Indian listeners pays roughly ₹0.06–₹0.08 per stream, so a million streams earns approximately ₹60,000–₹80,000 before the distributor's cut. The artists building sustainable income from Indian indie are doing it through live performance, brand deals, and sync licensing, not streaming. A label deal does not change those numbers in your favour.

For artists who want this thinking applied directly to their situation, we run the Artist Development Programme together, with a small number of artists at a time. Everything you build, you keep.
