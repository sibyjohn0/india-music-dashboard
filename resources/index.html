The reading list

Selected by Siby John and Karishma Changroth, not aggregated or sponsored. The criterion is simple: does this change how an independent Indian artist thinks about their career. The list grows when something worth adding appears, not on a schedule.

Foundations
How audiences actually work
The essays that reframe what it means to build a following. Read these before you think about streaming numbers.
01
1,000 True Fans
Kevin Kelly · The Technium · 2008
The original argument that you don't need millions of listeners, you need a small number of people who will buy everything you make. Still the most important frame for independent artists building from scratch.
→
02
1,000 True Fans? Try 100
Li Jin · Andreessen Horowitz · 2020
The sharper update. Kelly's math assumed a direct financial relationship with fans that streaming has made harder. Li Jin argues that 100 people who pay you directly is more achievable and more sustainable than chasing 1,000.
→
03
Why 1,000 True Fans Isn't Enough in 2024
Hypebot · 2024
The honest critique. Kelly's framework was built before passive streaming listeners existed. This piece does the real math on what a true fan actually spends today and what that means for the economics.
→
The Indian scene
How Indian indie actually works
Pieces specific to the Indian market. The context most advice from the US or UK misses entirely.
04
Ritviz On How To Start A Career In The Independent Music Scene
Homegrown
One of the few interviews where a successful Indian indie artist talks honestly about the early decisions: what worked, what didn't, and how the business side actually operates from the inside. More useful than any overview.
→
05
How India's Independent Music Landscape Has Evolved
Red Bull India
The longer arc, where Indian indie came from, what changed with streaming and social, and what the scene looks like now. Good for understanding why the current moment is different from five years ago.
→
06
The People Forging A Brave New Future For India's Alternative Music Landscape
Homegrown
Portraits of the people building the infrastructure around Indian indie: labels, bookers, curators, platforms. Useful for understanding who the ecosystem actually depends on beyond the artists themselves.
→
07
An Insider's Guide To The Best Indian Venues For Independent Music
Homegrown
Practical and specific. The venues that matter in each city, what each one looks for, and how to approach them. Read this before you approach any booker.
→
08
Why Global Success For Indian Indie May Look Nothing Like K-Pop
The Federal
A sober read on what Indian indie's global moment could actually look like, and why the K-pop comparison is the wrong frame. Important for artists building with international ambition.
→
09
Cultural Imperialism or Collaboration? Western Influences on the Indian Indie Scene
The Indian Music Diaries
The identity question every Indian indie artist faces eventually: am I making something distinctly mine, or imitating a template? The piece argues Indian artists are actively translating Western forms, not just consuming them. Useful for thinking about what your sound is actually doing.
→
The business
Money, rights, and contracts
The practical stuff most artists learn too late. Read these before you sign anything or release anything.
10
How Music Royalties Work In India: A Practical Guide
Tunevalley
The clearest explanation of how royalties actually flow in India: performance, mechanical, sync. Covers IPRS and what you should be collecting that you probably aren't. Start here on the money side.
→
11
Signing Contracts: A Short Guide To Artist Rights, Contract Clauses And More
The Indian Music Diaries
What to look for, what to push back on, and what the standard clauses actually mean in an Indian context. Read this before anyone puts a contract in front of you.
→
12
Who Gets Paid For The Music You Listen To?
SpicyIP · India's leading IP law blog
The actual payment chain in Indian music, traced carefully. The finding: of every ₹100 a listener pays, roughly ₹8.75 reaches the composers and lyricists. The rest goes to platforms, labels, and publishers. Read this to understand why the streaming numbers your distributor shows you tell only part of the story.
→
The platform question
What streaming is actually doing
Not why streaming is bad, but why understanding how it works changes how you use it.
13
Spotify Commissioned Cheap "Fake Artists" And She's Got the Proof
Ari Herstand · Ari's Take · 2024
Covers Liz Pelly's investigation into Spotify's Perfect Fit Content programme, exploring how Spotify fills mood playlists with cheap commissioned tracks instead of independent artists. The Harper's original is paywalled. This is the full story, free.
→
Newsletters
Worth following, not just reading once
Substacks that think seriously about music as a business and a culture. Free tiers on all of these.
14
Music x Neon
Substack · India-focused
One of the few newsletters specifically covering the Indian music ecosystem: new-age companies, independent labels, the infrastructure being built around indie. The overview of the Indian music ecosystem is the piece to start with.
→
15
Penny Fractions
David Turner · Substack
The most rigorous writing on music economics from an artist-first perspective. Covers streaming revenue, platform economics, and what the numbers actually mean for working musicians. Not cheerleading, but honest analysis.
→
16
Wild City
Wild City · India
The closest thing Indian indie has to a paper of record. New releases, scene features, interviews, and cultural coverage. The site that has tracked Indian alternative music longer and more seriously than anyone else.
→
