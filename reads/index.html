The reading list.

Not everything — the things worth reading. Essays, features, and guides that change how you think about building a career in independent music. Kept short by design.

Foundations
How audiences actually work
The essays that reframe what it means to build a following. Read these before you think about streaming numbers.
01
1,000 True Fans
Kevin Kelly · The Technium · 2008
The original argument that you don't need millions of listeners — you need a small number of people who will buy everything you make. Still the most important frame for independent artists building from scratch.
→
02
1,000 True Fans? Try 100
Li Jin · Andreessen Horowitz · 2020
The sharper update. Kelly's math assumed a direct financial relationship with fans that streaming has made harder. Li Jin argues that 100 people who pay you directly is more achievable and more sustainable than chasing 1,000.
→
03
Why 1,000 True Fans Isn't Enough in 2024
Hypebot · 2024
The honest critique. Kelly's framework was built before passive streaming listeners existed. This piece does the real math on what a true fan actually spends today and what that means for the economics.
→
The Indian scene
How Indian indie actually works
Pieces specific to the Indian market. The context most advice from the US or UK misses entirely.
04
Indie 101: A Guide To The Independent Music Industry In India
Wild City
The clearest overview of how the Indian indie ecosystem actually works — who the players are, how distribution works locally, and what the entry points look like. Start here if you're new to the scene.
→
05
How India's Independent Music Landscape Has Evolved
Red Bull India
The longer arc — where Indian indie came from, what changed with streaming and social, and what the scene looks like now. Good for understanding why the current moment is different from five years ago.
→
06
The People Forging A Brave New Future For India's Alternative Music Landscape
Homegrown
Portraits of the people building the infrastructure around Indian indie — labels, bookers, curators, platforms. Useful for understanding who the ecosystem actually depends on beyond the artists themselves.
→
07
An Insider's Guide To The Best Indian Venues For Independent Music
Homegrown
Practical and specific. The venues that matter in each city, what each one looks for, and how to approach them. Read this before you approach any booker.
→
08
Why Global Success For Indian Indie May Look Nothing Like K-Pop
The Federal
A sober read on what Indian indie's global moment could actually look like — and why the K-pop comparison is the wrong frame. Important for artists building with international ambition.
→
The business
Money, rights, and contracts
The practical stuff most artists learn too late. Read these before you sign anything or release anything.
09
How Music Royalties Work In India: A Practical Guide
Tunevalley
The clearest explanation of how royalties actually flow in India — performance, mechanical, sync. Covers IPRS and what you should be collecting that you probably aren't. Start here on the money side.
→
10
Signing Contracts: A Short Guide To Artist Rights, Contract Clauses And More
The Indian Music Diaries
What to look for, what to push back on, and what the standard clauses actually mean in an Indian context. Read this before anyone puts a contract in front of you.
→
The platform question
What streaming is actually doing
Not why streaming is bad — why understanding how it works changes how you use it.
11
The Ghosts in the Machine
Liz Pelly · Harper's Magazine · December 2024
The most important piece on Spotify in years. Pelly documents how Spotify commissions cheap anonymous tracks to fill mood playlists — pushing out independent artists. If you've ever wondered why the algorithm doesn't find you, read this.
