Why I'm building this
The problem with music advice in India
Siby John

India has 204 million music streamers and one of the fastest-growing independent scenes anywhere in the world. But the infrastructure for the artists making that music barely exists. Labels are opaque. Platforms are algorithmic black boxes. Most advice available online is written for the Western market and does not account for IPRS, JioSaavn, the NRI diaspora market, or the tier-2 city live boom that is reshaping where money actually comes from.

I spent time mapping how the independent music economy actually works in India — who the real gatekeepers are, what the revenue numbers look like in practice, what platforms actually pay per stream, which curators genuinely surface new artists versus which ones are pay-to-play. The tools here are the output of that research, built openly so any artist can use them without hiring someone to explain it.

The honest version: streaming pays about ₹10,000–16,500 per million plays in India. The artists who build real incomes are not the most talented ones. They are the most consistent, the most strategic, and the most specific about who they are. This is a system to help serious artists become those things.

Everything here is free. The only thing I ask is that you use it.
